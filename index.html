Mission Statement

What We Do

Stocks for Charity is a byproduct of our pursuit of using investments for the community’s benefit. Our method of contributions involves using our portfolio to build up funds, which will then be used to facilitate donations to our chosen recipient, Hillsides Education Center in Pasadena. On a daily basis, we day trade on the stock market, and on a per-month basis, evaluate and determine a reasonable profit margin based on performance.

Donations

Since COVID-19’s impact constituted our account’s company offices to temporarily close, withdrawing funds physically from the account is an unavailable option. As such, all profits earned from the account will stay in the account to continue generating revenue. However, we will still meet 1-2 months in pledged donations, which we will use to buy necessary supplies to aid Hillsides in developing their kids’ educational and personal lives. For instance, in a 1 ½ month period in which the portfolio produces a $1000 gain, anywhere from 20% - 40% of those gains will be used as a donation. Additionally, donation periods can be staggered so that a combined donation will be larger than individual contributions. Hillsides have designated on its website a list of items that they have demonstrated a need for. This includes e-gift cards, board games, Legos, science kits, and other items that will enrich the kids’ experience.

Our Message

As both of us are heavily involved in education, hence Stocks for Charity, we want to not only give back to the community through donations but also our investment experiences. One of the main hurdles that youth have to overcome is financial literacy, which often leads to unpreparedness later on in life. Some prevalent trends of this widespread problem include relying on governmental aid in retirement, or not managing money wisely early on in life, and not being briefed on how strategically investing will prepare individuals for tough times. By showing the thought processes behind our trades, we strive to show viewers an inside look at how we invest in the community.

Hillsides

Overview

Situated between the contrastingly diverse Los Angeles areas of South Pasadena, Highland Park, and Eagle Rock, Hillsides Education Center serves the surrounding community by providing refuge and education to those who don’t have access to it. They house young individuals who aren’t fortunate enough to do so at home, work on developing their kids in their education, but most of all, are there to support and create a lasting change for America’s future. Their network of staff, contributors, and volunteers work tirelessly around the clock to give back and propel the youth to unparalleled heights. They focus solely on putting children first, and “envision a world in which children and young adults, families, and communities are able to heal, grow, and thrive”. From our connection in sharing the same ideologies and personally having experience in what Hillsides strives to do, we thought it only right to help as much as we could in upholding those values. Especially now, where COVID-19 has hurt all businesses, non-profit organizations/education centers such as Hillsides are hurting most. This is due to the fact that their main source of income is through donations, so we must continue to work to provide them with as much aid as we can.

Their Services

Hillsides is most notably known for their education center, where they take in an array of underprivileged students from local school districts. They are a therapeutic residential and day school that offers individualized education for students experiencing social-emotional, learning, and/or behavior challenges. In addition, the staff help students develop personal and social awareness, tenacity, and problem-solving skills that help the kids build positive self-images. Upon first look of the campus, you might ask yourself, “Why is it so large?”. Certainly, the entire property isn’t only used education: Hillsides offers residential treatment services, which “provides a safe and stable environment in a short-term residential therapeutic program for children and youth who cannot live at home, who suffered trauma and who have severe emotional, psychological and behavioral challenges requiring specialized care and treatment”. Hillsides is a safe haven, with 5 cottages, a recreation and resource center, nursing offices, library, pool, playground, and countless other beneficiary establishments. Along with full development of the kids, Hillsides works to help them move on in life. They provide a support system that includes a network of assisting adults, training, paid internships, and other infrastructure designed to help the kids go far.
